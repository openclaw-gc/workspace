Global Nuclear Capacity: 440 reactors, 414 GW installed capacity, generating 2,667 TWh annually (~9% of global electricity, ~20% of low-carbon power).

Key Markets:

  • US: 93 GW (27% of US low-carbon power) — aging fleet but policy turning bullish
  • France: 63 GW (67% of electricity) — committing to 6-14 new large reactors by 2050
  • China: 59 GW (37 reactors under construction) — dominant growth engine, 4.7% of generation today → targeting 10%+ by 2035
  • Emerging: India (24 reactors), South Korea (26 reactors), UAE (first Arab nuclear nation)

Under Construction: 60+ reactors globally (led by China, India, Russia, South Korea)

New Commitments (2022-2025):

  • US: Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) production tax credits for nuclear (up to $15/MWh)
  • UK: 24 GW target by 2050 (8 large reactors + SMRs)
  • France: 6 new EPR reactors + 8 optional (€50B commitment)
  • Japan: Fleet restart + lifetime extensions + new builds (reversing Fukushima phase-out)
  • Poland: First 3 AP1000s (Westinghouse) + APR1400s (KEPCO)
  • Canada: SMR commercialization pathway + 30% ITC for clean tech including SMRs

COP28 Nuclear Pledge (Dec 2023): 25+ countries committed to tripling nuclear capacity by 2050. Unprecedented multilateral endorsement.

Structural Drivers (Why Now?)

1. AI Data Center Power Demand (The New Killer App)

The Problem:

  • AI training and inference workloads are power-intensive (GPT-4 training: ~50 MW sustained for months)
  • Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) projecting 100-300 GW of incremental data center demand by 2030
  • Grid constraints in key markets (Virginia, Singapore, Dublin) limiting expansion
  • Solar/wind intermittency incompatible with 24/7 AI compute requirements

The Nuclear Solution:

  • Baseload, carbon-free, high-capacity factor (90%+)
  • Direct colocation at nuclear sites (Microsoft/Constellation Energy: Three Mile Island restart for 835 MW dedicated to Azure)
  • Google: 500 MW Kairos SMR deal (molten salt reactors, first deployment 2030)
  • Amazon: 300 MW X-Energy SMR investment + Talen Energy data center colocation at Susquehanna nuclear plant

Impact: Nuclear transitions from utility commodity to premium power product for tech. Pricing power shifts from regulated tariffs to private PPAs at premium rates.

2. Grid Decarbonization Limits (The Intermittency Wall)

The Reality Check:

  • Solar/wind penetration hitting diminishing returns at 40-60% of grid mix (storage economics break)
  • California rolling blackouts (2020, 2022) exposed intermittency risk
  • Europe energy crisis (2022) revealed fossil dependency despite renewables buildout
  • "Clean firm power" gap: Need 300+ GW globally by 2035 to complement renewables

Nuclear's Role:

  • Only proven technology delivering gigawatt-scale, carbon-free, dispatchable power
  • Complements renewables (provides grid stability, frequency regulation, voltage support)
  • SMRs enable flexible deployment (100-300 MW modules, scalable, load-following capable)

IEA Net Zero Scenario: Nuclear must grow 3% annually (15 GW/year) to 2030 to stay on track. Current pace: 7.9 GW/year (2022). Gap = investment opportunity.

3. Energy Security & Geopolitics

Post-Ukraine War Paradigm Shift:

  • Europe's Russian gas dependency catalyzed nuclear reassessment (Germany reversing phase-out, Belgium extending reactors)
  • France positioning nuclear as "strategic sovereignty" pillar
  • US-China decoupling driving domestic supply chain onshoring (uranium, enrichment, fuel fabrication)

Uranium Market Dynamics:

  • Supply: Concentrated (Kazakhstan 43%, Canada 15%, Australia 12%) — geopolitical risk premium rising
  • Demand: China building 37 reactors, US extending fleet lifetimes + new builds
  • Supply Deficit Emerging: Mine supply ~140M lbs U3O8/year, reactor demand ~180M lbs (deficit covered by secondary supply, but inventory depleting)

4. SMR Commercialization (The iPhone Moment)

Why SMRs Matter:

  • Factory-built: Modular construction → lower cost, faster deployment, reduced project risk
  • Smaller scale: 10-300 MW (vs. 1,000+ MW conventional) → fits distributed grids, industrial applications, remote sites
  • Passive safety: Advanced designs eliminate accident scenarios (no Fukushima risk)
  • Fuel flexibility: Some designs run on spent fuel (waste recycling), thorium, or HALEU (high-assay low-enriched uranium)

Commercial Traction:

  • NuScale (SMR): First US NRC-approved SMR design, UAMPS project (Utah) targeting 2029
  • Oklo (Aurora): Fast reactor, 1.5 MW micro-reactors for data centers/military, going public via SPAC
  • X-Energy (Xe-100): High-temp gas reactor, Amazon-backed, targeting 2030 deployment
  • TerraPower (Natrium): Bill Gates-backed, sodium-cooled, Wyoming demo project 2030
  • Rolls-Royce SMR (UK): 470 MW design, £20B pipeline, Czech Republic + Poland interest

Inflection Point: First commercial SMRs operational 2027-2030. If successful, unlocks $500B+ addressable market (IEA estimate).

5. Policy & Financing Shift

US Inflation Reduction Act (2022):

  • Production Tax Credit (PTC): $15-25/MWh for new nuclear (makes economics competitive with gas)
  • Advanced Manufacturing Credits: 30% for domestic fuel cycle components
  • DOE Loan Guarantees: Restarted for new nuclear projects

EU Taxonomy Inclusion (2022):

  • Nuclear classified as "green investment" → unlocks ESG capital (~$35T AUM)
  • Removes stigma blocking pension funds, sovereign wealth funds

COP28 Pledge (2023):

  • 25 countries commit to tripling nuclear by 2050
  • World Bank, EBRD reconsidering nuclear financing bans

Net Impact: Cost of capital for nuclear projects declining (15%+ WACC → 8-10% with policy support). Game-changer for NPV economics.

Investment Thematics

Theme 1: Uranium Supply Squeeze (Highest Conviction)

Thesis: Structural deficit emerging as reactor restarts + new builds collide with underinvested mine supply. Uranium spot price ($80-90/lb today) needs $100-120/lb to incentivize new production.

Catalysts:

  • Kazakhstan (43% of global supply) production plateauing
  • Cameco/McArthur River restart not enough to cover deficit
  • US/EU banning Russian uranium imports (20% of global supply)
  • Utilities shifting from spot purchases to long-term contracts (securing 10-year supply)

Plays:

  • Cameco (CCJ): Tier-1 producer, McArthur River (world's largest mine), Cigar Lake, conversion/refining assets. Liquid, institutional quality.
  • Kazatomprom (KAP.L): State-owned Kazakh producer, 43% market share. Commodity leverage play.
  • Sprott Physical Uranium Trust (U.UN / SRUUF): Physical uranium ETF, removes supply from spot market. Pure commodity exposure.
  • Energy Fuels (UUUU): US producer, rare earth diversification, DOE contracts. Small cap, volatile, high beta.

Price Targets (12-18mo):

  • Uranium spot: $110-130/lb (from $85 today)
  • CCJ: $65-75 (from $55 today) — 18-36% upside
  • U.UN: Tracks spot uranium (30-50% upside if thesis plays out)

Theme 2: Utility Nuclear Renaissance (Value + Growth Hybrid)

Thesis: Existing nuclear operators benefit from (1) fleet life extensions, (2) uprates, (3) premium PPA pricing (data centers), (4) new build optionality. Underpriced relative to growth trajectory.

Plays:

  • Constellation Energy (CEG): Largest US nuclear fleet (21 GW), Microsoft PPA for Three Mile Island restart, SMR development pipeline. Core holding.
  • Électricité de France (EDF.PA): French incumbent, 56 reactors, government backing 6-14 new EPRs. Sovereign nuclear champion.
  • Entergy (ETR): US Southern nuclear operator, strong regulatory relationships, dividend yield. Defensive income + growth.

Catalysts:

  • Fleet life extensions (40 → 60 → 80 years) with minimal capex
  • Capacity uprates (5-20% output increases via turbine/generator upgrades)
  • Data center PPAs at $60-80/MWh (vs. $40/MWh regulated tariffs)

Valuation: CEG trading at 12x P/E, 8% FCF yield. Comp: Renewable developers at 25-30x. Re-rating potential.

Theme 3: SMR/Next-Gen Tech (Venture/Growth, High Risk-Reward)

Thesis: SMRs are the "shale revolution" of nuclear — unlock new markets (data centers, industrial heat, remote grids, developing nations). Early movers capture asymmetric upside.

Plays:

  • NuScale Power (SMR): Public (SMFR), first NRC-approved SMR, UAMPS project. Near-term revenue visibility.
  • Oklo Inc. (OKLO): Fast reactor micro-SMRs, backed by Sam Altman, DoE site agreements. High beta, binary outcomes.
  • TerraPower (private): Bill Gates-backed, Natrium SMR, Wyoming demo. Access via private funds or wait for IPO.
  • X-Energy (private): Amazon-backed, Xe-100 high-temp gas reactor. Access via private funds or secondary.
  • BWX Technologies (BWXT): Public, supplies naval reactors + SMR components. Picks-and-shovels play.

Risks:

  • Regulatory delays: NRC approval timelines uncertain
  • First-of-a-kind risk: Construction/cost overruns
  • Technology unproven at scale: Early SMRs may face teething issues

Upside Case: If SMRs deliver on cost/speed promises, $500B TAM unlocks. Early winners could be 10-50x over decade.

Theme 4: Enrichment & Fuel Cycle (Oligopoly, Strategic)

Thesis: Enrichment capacity constrained (Russia 40% market share, US/EU scrambling to onshore). SMRs require HALEU (high-assay LEU), zero commercial supply today. Bottleneck = pricing power.

Plays:

  • Centrus Energy (LEU): Only US company producing HALEU (DoE contract), small cap, volatile. High beta on HALEU demand.
  • Urenco (private, consortium-owned): EU enrichment champion, expanding capacity. Access via parent companies or bonds.
  • Cameco (CCJ): Owns conversion facilities (Port Hope, Blind River). Dual exposure: uranium + fuel services.

Catalysts:

  • US HALEU production ramp: DoE committing $2.7B to domestic supply chain
  • Russian enrichment ban: Pending US/EU legislation (20-40% supply offline)
  • SMR fuel contracts: X-Energy, NuScale, TerraPower all need HALEU

Risk Factors

Regulatory & Political Risks

  • NRC approval delays: Historically slow, risk to SMR timelines
  • State-level opposition: Some US states (e.g., CA, NY) remain anti-nuclear despite federal support
  • Waste disposal: No permanent US repository (Yucca Mountain stalled) — public perception risk

Execution Risks

  • Construction cost overruns: Vogtle 3&4 (Georgia) were 2x budget, 7 years late
  • First-of-a-kind SMR risk: No proven cost/schedule track record yet
  • Operator competence: Requires skilled workforce (shortage in US/EU)

Market Risks

  • Renewables + storage cost curve: If battery costs collapse faster than expected, nuclear value proposition weakens
  • Natural gas price volatility: Low gas prices (e.g., US shale glut) make nuclear less competitive
  • Uranium price risk: If supply responds faster than expected, commodity rally stalls

Black Swans

  • Accident: Any Fukushima-scale event reverses sentiment overnight
  • Geopolitical escalation: Ukraine war involving nuclear facilities, China-Taiwan over reactors

Mitigation: Diversify across sub-themes (upstream/midstream/downstream), overweight liquid names (CCJ, CEG), limit SMR exposure to <20% of thematic allocation.
