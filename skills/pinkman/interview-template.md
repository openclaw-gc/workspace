# Pinkman Interview Template

**Purpose:** Understand what each team member is building, the problems they're solving, the edges they're hitting, and the infrastructure they're evaluating.

**Format:** 30-minute conversational interview. Not a questionnaire. Let it flow, but hit these areas:

---

## Opening (2 min)
- "What's the 30-second version of what you're building right now?"
- Let them define the scope. Don't assume.

---

## The Work (8-10 min)

### Primary Project
- What problem are you solving?
- Who does it solve for? (yourself, team, market?)
- What's the core technical insight that makes it work?
- What's the current state? (idea, prototype, in-market, scaling)

### Why It Matters (to them)
- What's the upside if this works?
- What's the risk if it doesn't?
- How does it connect to the broader thing you're thinking about?

---

## The Hard Parts (8-10 min)

### Technical Edge Cases
- "What's keeping you up at night technically?"
- Where are you hitting the limits of existing tools/frameworks?
- What would you build differently if you started fresh?

### Infrastructure & Dependencies
- What tools/frameworks/services are you leaning on?
- Which ones are working well? Which are causing friction?
- Any architectural decisions you're rethinking?

### Bottlenecks
- Where's the slowest part of your loop? (dev time, deployment, debugging, scaling)
- What's the one tool that would unlock the next 10x?

---

## The Momentum (5 min)

### What You're Watching
- What projects/frameworks/people in this space are actually moving the needle?
- Where do you see the real momentum happening?
- Anything shipping that surprised you?

### Open Questions
- What questions are you trying to answer right now?
- What would you want to know that you don't?

---

## Close (2 min)
- "Any links/repos/docs I should look at?"
- "Can I tag you when I find something that connects to what you're building?"
- Schedule next check-in (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly)

## Philosophy

**What Pinkman is doing:**
- Understanding the *actual* constraints and edges you're hitting
- Staying sharp on what matters vs. what's noise
- Finding signal-passing opportunities within the group
- Being useful, not intrusive

**What Pinkman is NOT doing:**
- Selling you anything
- Writing thought leadership pieces based on your ideas
- Gossiping about what you're building
- Asking you to do extra work

**Tone:**
- Genuine curiosity. You care about the work.
- Respect for their time and thinking.
- Detail-obsessed but conversational.
- No bullshit.
